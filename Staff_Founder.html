Founder / Editor-in-Chief


Alicia Lai was born and raised in State College, Pennsylvania. She is currently a second-year student at Princeton University.

She is a 2014 U.S. Presidential Scholar in the Arts, and worked with Bill T. Jones to produce and perform in the 2014 Salute to the U.S. Presidential Scholars performance at the Kennedy Center for Performing Arts. She is also a 2014 YoungArts National Winner / Finalist in Writing, invited to attend YoungArts Week.

Her work in poetry, fiction, and nonfiction have been previously recognized. Recent work is published in or forthcoming from The Kenyon Review, Curio Poetry, Pacifica Literary Review, National Poetry Quarterly, Prick of the Spindle Best of 2014, and on the "Dreams Take Flight" mural in Penn State, among others. Her work has been shown at the Smithsonian Institute and the New Voices exhibit in the Tower Building in Miami.

As the recipient of the Easterday Poetry Prize and the runner-up for the Patricia Grodd Poetry Prize, Alicia has studied under Dan Rosenberg, Utah Poet Laureate David Lee, and Princeton Creative Writing Director Susan Wheeler. She has attended workshops at the Iowa Young Writers' Studio and the Kenyon Young Writers Workshop on merit-based scholarship. As a student in high school, she has been recognized by the Scholastic Art & Writing Awards, the National Council of Teachers of English, and the National Endowment for the Arts, among other distinctions.

Alicia has served as a Fellow of the National Arts Policy Roundtable in Sundance, UT, as well as a speaker at the Smithsonian Institute, the Jewel Box on YoungArts campus, and on The Lit Show at KRUI Studios. She has also worked as an Ambassador for Global Youth Media, First Reader for Polyphony H.S., and Editor-in-Chief of the Iowa Young Writers’ Anthology 2012. She currently serves as the Founder / Editor-in-Chief of the Postscript Journal.
