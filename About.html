About

The Postscript Journal is a publication that I founded in 2012, publishing oustanding literary and artistic works by high school and college students in an in-print international journal. We are an entirely student-run publication for young writers.

As we receive hundreds of submissions each reading period with an approximate 15% acceptance rate, our staff of students has been an integral part of the process. The Postscript family now consists of talented young writers from across the United States, as well as from Russia, France, China, Nepal, Mauritius, and Morocco. From working with local students to corresponding with a Nobel Prize winner to affiliation with national literary organizations, the journal has certainly made an impact worldwide.

As a collective, our editors and readers have a love for the electric—looking at subject matter in an unusual, moving way. We’ll look at the standard poetry, fiction, and creative non-fiction, but we’ll also gladly read definitions, plays, and lists. The breathy, risky, strange; the insanely elliptical or the rapturously lyrical. We aim to output a journal of pieces that have never been seen before, each issue.

Because it’s important to ensure literature isn’t isolated from other fields, many of our issues will contain special features. For example, in our Spring 2013 issue, we worked with fourteen renowned writers and translators from Myanmar in a Burmese Feature to promote culture and new literature—for this, we corresponded with the Irrawaddy Literary Festival and Nobel Peace Prize Winner Aung San Suu Kyi. We’re also soon releasing an issue that will blend writing and art from student photographers, some whom have been featured in National Geographic and Vogue.

Postscript has also succeeded on the individual level, becoming “the journal of brave new energies unleashed,” says Dan Rosenberg, winner of the American Poetry Journal Book Prize, “these poems and stories vibrate with fearless life.”

We invite you to order your very own issue of Postscript, to support the future continuation of the journal and to develop a better understanding of what it is we aim to do.

We're looking forward to reading your work.
