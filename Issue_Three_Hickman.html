Issue Three


First Aid for Choking

Noah Hickman


Infants
Hold them by the belly and whisper and coax, foretelling their future. The blockage will feel obligated by your promise, and release itself.

Children
This age cannot choke on anything smaller than a bite of hot dog. For you, it is much like a carrot and stick. You must bring the crudely assembled device to be ominous (a pendulous state). The dilation of the throat matches the eyes, now wide as the palm of your dangling hand. The child is now free.

Adults
Hook your hand under the sternum and scream, threaten violence. Remember what you promised the infant, and threaten its revocation. Make them feel infantile. Grab their belly again, but make a fist. Remind them of your color, your position, your politics. Then use your free hand and grab their throat. Prepare for the violation of any surrounding surfaces.


Noah Hickman is a senior at American Fork High School in American Fork, Utah. He is a National First Reader for Polyphony HS and the president of his high school's poetry club. He plans on serving a two year mission for the LDS church following his high school graduation. He intends on majoring in either English or Philosophy/Religion and hopes to attend Brigham Young University for all of his undergraduate work.
